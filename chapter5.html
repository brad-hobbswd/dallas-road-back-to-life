Chapter Five

The Mountain of Freedom

Freedom often waits on the other side of surrender.

The trail rose sharply, winding toward a mountain that seemed to pierce the sky. Dallas looked up at the peak, chest tightening with anticipation. The climb ahead was steep, rocky, and uncertain.

Every muscle in his legs ached already, yet he knew this mountain represented more than a physical challenge. This was the place where the deepest battles within him would surface.

He paused at the base and remembered the words of Solomon and Micah. Temptation, fear, doubt. Those voices would still exist. God's presence would also remain, steadfast and powerful.

Dallas bowed his head and whispered:

"Lord, I want to climb this mountain with You."

I want to leave the old life behind. Help me take one step at a time. I choose You. I choose You completely.

He began to climb. Jagged rocks forced careful placement of each foot. The wind whipped against his face, cold and relentless, testing his resolve. Halfway up, he stumbled on a loose stone and fell to his hands and knees.

His heart raced. Thoughts of failure surged through his mind.

You cannot do this. You always fall. You will go back.

Dallas pressed his palms against the rock and closed his eyes in prayer.

Lord, I cannot do this alone. Strengthen me. Steady me. Fill me with Your courage.

A calm washed over him. He felt God's presence surrounding him, steadying his hands and filling his lungs with confidence that was not his own. Rising slowly, he gripped the rocks with renewed determination and continued upward.

As he climbed, memories of his past addiction came sharply to mind. Nights of hiding, lies, broken relationships, and shame that had defined him returned one by one. Every memory pressed like added weight, threatening to push him backward.

Dallas refused to let those memories control him. Instead, he whispered each one to God and surrendered them completely.

With each step, he felt stronger. Every movement refined him. Every breath filled him with greater clarity. The mountain was not only a physical test. The mountain had become a spiritual one. Every stumble, every fear, and every memory from the past became an opportunity to lean fully upon God.

Near the summit, the wind grew fierce. Clouds swirled around him and the narrow path felt dangerously thin. His body screamed for rest. His mind whispered another lie.

You have come this far, but you are not strong enough to finish. Turn back.

Dallas pressed his hands against his chest and whispered:

No. I am not strong, but You are. I am not perfect, but You are. I choose You over everything else.

A surge of peace filled him, stronger than the wind, brighter than the morning sun, and steadier than the rocks beneath his feet. In that moment, Dallas realized he had spent years double minded, torn between addiction and God, fear and faith, shame and hope.

At that summit, he chose with one mind and one heart.

He chose God.

Step by step, Dallas reached the top. Standing at the summit, he looked across the valley below. Sunrise broke through the clouds with brilliant streaks of gold, illuminating every path he had traveled. Rivers, valleys, nights of doubt, and moments of surrender had all led him to this place.

Tears streamed down his face. Relief alone could not explain what he felt.

This was freedom.

This was victory.

This was the unshakable peace of a heart fully surrendered to God.

The climb had never been about escaping his old life. The climb had been about learning how to stand firmly in God's love with a single mind and a steady heart.

Dallas knelt and lifted his hands toward heaven.

Lord, thank You. Thank You for carrying me when I was weak. Thank You for fighting for me when I could not fight for myself. I belong to You. I am Yours.

In that moment, the chains of his past finally fell away. Addiction, once his master, now felt distant and powerless. His heart no longer wavered. His mind no longer wavered. His ways became steady and anchored in God's love.

A gentle wind moved across the summit and carried a whisper through the clouds. Dallas could not explain the feeling, yet he knew what he sensed.

You are free. Walk in this freedom and help others find theirs.

Dallas smiled as the weight of his old life lifted completely. Mountains had tested him. Rivers had attempted to pull him backward. Valleys had challenged every part of his resolve.

Yet he had chosen God with one mind and one heart, and every step had carried him toward victory.

For the first time in years, Dallas walked without fear, without doubt, and without shame. Peace, strength, and purpose guided him now. The road ahead would still require faith, though God's love had become the foundation beneath every step.

Dallas had reached the top.

He was free. He was steady. He was unshakable.
