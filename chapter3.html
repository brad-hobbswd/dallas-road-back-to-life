Chapter Three

The War Within

The loudest battles are often fought where nobody else can see.

Morning arrived quietly, yet inside Dallas, a storm raged. The sun rose gently, painting the sky in soft blues and lavender, but his mind moved in restless circles. He sat at the edge of his mattress, elbows on his knees, palms covering his face.

Last night had offered a glimpse of peace, yet the craving for drugs still pulled at him, insistent and familiar, like an old friend knocking on the door of his soul.

He tried to pray, but the words scattered. Opening his Bible, he read the verse again:

"A double minded man is unstable in all his ways."

The words followed him like shadows, echoing through every part of him. He knew he stood at a crossroad. One path led toward God and the life he had begun to sense. The other pulled him back into the shadows he knew all too well.
